Bond Investment Education

What is a Bond?

A bond is a type of debt security in which the issuer owes the holders a debt and is obliged to pay interest (the coupon) and/or to repay the principal at a later date, termed the maturity date.

How Do Bonds Work?

When you purchase a bond, you are essentially lending money to the issuer. In return, you receive regular interest payments (called coupons) and your principal is repaid at maturity. In the case of government bonds, these payments are typically guaranteed by the government.

Why Invest in Bonds?

Bonds provide a relatively safe investment with fixed returns. They can help diversify your investment portfolio and reduce overall risk. Additionally, Zambian government bonds are a good way to earn interest while contributing to national development.
